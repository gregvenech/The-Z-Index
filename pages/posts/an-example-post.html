title: Introduction author: Greg Venech date: 15-10-2015 headline: What we're about... {% extends "post.html" %} {% block body %} {% filter markdown %} In today's web development world, there are a vast number of tools to choose from when creating a project. Pick too many and you risk creating a bloated app/site. Pick too few and you end up reinventing the wheel at every turn. Finding the right balance can be hard, especially under the time constraints many of us face. It is my hope that this site will alleviate at least some of that pain. First some background. Satish and I, Greg, are both avid developers with an shared interest in tinkering around on the web. We hope to accomplish a few things with this site. First, we'd like to pass on some of the knowledge we've picked up building data-driven web applications and other web projects. I'm sure most of you have heard the following quote or something similar... > You never really learn something until you teach it. I can't count the number of times I've gone to explain something I use everyday and realize halfway through the first sentence that I'm clueless as to the why it works the way it does. So not only are we trying to share the techniques we've picked up, but are confident that writing each post will give us a deeper understanding the topic at hand. Secondly, remember... this is a list, we will do our best to provide real world examples and scenarios. This is not to say that we won't use [link to json generator or something]mock data at times but some context can definitely enrich the process. Each post [link to RFC defining must etc.] WILL be accompanied with a means to deconstruct the component under discussion. For example, anything to do with HTML, CSS, and JS will be accompanied by a [link to codepen] pen. If it's re a design, the PSD and any other [link to various .aco etc.]accompanying files. You get the point... Finally we (?) also have some grandiose plans to integrate many of the things we discuss into a funky, feature-rich [link to github]mega-app. This is MOST DEFINITELY NOT to encourage [feature bloat article]feature bloat in your applications, but, as mentioned, to provide a semi-real world context for the things we discuss. At the end of the day, I really can't say for sure what this site will or won't be. Don't take anything we say as [link]scripture and feel free to fact-check us and call us out when necessary. We're just two more [http://www.nytimes.com/2015/10/11/opinion/sunday/what-really-keeps-women-out-of-tech.html?_r=0]guys staring at the debugger, trying to figure out what da fook is wrong with our code...
